# Business Insights — RFM Analysis

## 1. Revenue Concentration

Revenue is highly concentrated in the VIP segment, indicating that a small group of customers drives a large portion of total sales.

---

## 2. High-Value but Infrequent Buyers

Big Spenders have the highest average customer value but lower purchase frequency, suggesting opportunities for increasing engagement.

---

## 3. At-Risk Customers Hold Value

The At Risk segment still contributes meaningful revenue, indicating that previously valuable customers are becoming inactive.

This group represents a key opportunity for retention strategies.

---

## 4. Low Contribution from Lost Customers

Lost customers show minimal revenue contribution and low engagement across all metrics.

This segment has limited short-term business impact.

---

## 5. Segment Imbalance

A large portion of customers fall into Regular and low-value segments, while high-value segments are relatively small.

This indicates strong revenue dependency on a limited customer base.

---

## 6. Clear Behavioral Separation

RFM scoring successfully differentiates customer groups:

- VIP → high across all metrics  
- Loyal → frequent but moderate value  
- At Risk → low recency but strong past behavior  

This confirms that the segmentation logic is effective.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights that revenue is driven by a small group of high-value customers, while several segments present opportunities for retention and growth strategies.
